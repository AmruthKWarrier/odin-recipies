How to make Sambar

Sāmbār is a lentil-based vegetable spiced curry or stew, cooked with pigeon pea and tamarind broth. It originates in South Indian cuisine and is popular in other parts of India.


In southern states of India namely Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Kerala and Tamil Nadu, Sambar is made using different vegetables along with lentils or coconut. Each region has its own version of making sambar, even though the same vegetables are used, the condiments and ingredients, and the method differ regionwise. In Tulu speaking areas of coastal Karnataka coconut is predominantly used to prepare Koddel (Sambar), The grated coconut is ground with spices to form a paste which is added to vegetables boiled. In Kannada speaking regions sambar is referred to as Saaru.

Ingredients

  • Lentils
  • Mixed vegetables
  • Tamarind
  • Sambar Masala
  • Mustard

Steps

  1. Cook the Dal
  2. Prepare Tamarind
  3. Cook the Vegetables
  4. Add Tamarind & Sambar Powder
  5. Add Cooked Dal
  6. Prepare the Tempering
  7. Combine & Finish
  8. Garnish & Serve
